Epipremnum, Philodendron, and Scindapsus are all popular choices for indoor plants, known for their lush foliage and air-purifying qualities. However, these plants belong to different genera and have distinct characteristics that can help you tell them apart. Here's a quick guide on how to differentiate these green beauties.

Leaves and Leaf Shape

One of the most noticeable differences is in the shape of their leaves. Epipremnum (commonly known as Pothos) typically has heart-shaped leaves with prominent marbling. Philodendron leaves can vary but are often broader with smooth edges. Scindapsus leaves tend to be more elongated and arrow-shaped.

Leaf Coloration

Leaf coloration can also be a distinguishing feature. Epipremnum leaves are typically variegated with shades of green and yellow or white. Philodendron leaves can be solid green or variegated, often with shades of green or cream. Scindapsus leaves are typically green with silver or silvery-white markings.

Growth Habit

The growth habit of these plants can differ. Epipremnum tends to be a trailing or climbing plant, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained to climb. Philodendron plants can have a climbing or upright growth habit, and some varieties are bushy. Scindapsus is often a climbing or trailing plant with long vines.

Care Requirements

While care requirements can vary within each genus, knowing the specific care needs of your plant can help identify it. Epipremnum (Pothos) is known for its low-maintenance and forgiving nature. Philodendrons may have varying care requirements, and some prefer bright, indirect light. Scindapsus also thrives in low to moderate light conditions.
